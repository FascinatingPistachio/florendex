Summer Snowflake

Leucojum aestivum

Leucojum aestivum, commonly called the summer snowflake, giant snowflake, or Loddon lily, is a plant species widely cultivated as an ornamental. It is native to most of Europe from Spain and Ireland to Ukraine, with the exception of Scandinavia, Russia, Belarus and the Baltic countries. It is also considered native to Turkey, Iran and the Caucasus.

It has naturalized in several other parts of the world, including Denmark, South Australia, New South Wales, Nova Scotia and much of the eastern United States.

Description

Leucojum aestivum is a perennial bulbous plant, generally 35–60 cm (14–24 in) tall, but some forms reach up to 90 cm (35 in). Its leaves, which are well developed at the time of flowering, are strap-shaped and reach to about the same height as the flowers.

The flowering stem (scape) is hollow and has wings with translucent margins. The pendant flowers appear in late spring and are borne in umbels of usually three to five, sometimes as many as seven. The flowers are about 3–4 cm (1.2–1.6 in) in diameter and have six white tepals, each with a greenish mark just below the tip. The black seeds are 5–7 mm (0.2–0.3 in) long.

After flowering, the fruits develop flotation chambers but remain attached to the stem. Flooding can cause the stems to break and the fruits to be carried downstream and stranded in river debris or on flood-plains. The bulbs can also be transported during heavy floods and deposited on river banks.

Taxonomy

Leucojum aestivum was first described by Carl Linnaeus in 1759. The Latin specific epithet aestivum means "of the summer". Two subspecies have been recognized: the nominate L. aestivum subsp. aestivum and L. aestivum subsp. pulchellum, the latter being differentiated by its generally smaller dimensions and fewer flowers per stem.

Habitat & Cultivation

In the wild, L. aestivum is found in damp places, such as wet meadows, swamps, and ditches. When cultivated as an ornamental plant for its flowers, it requires a damp position, growing well on clay soils where it increases rapidly.

Notable cultivars include 'Gravetye Giant', a robust form growing to 90 cm with up to eight flowers on each scape which has gained the Royal Horticultural Society's Award of Garden Merit, and 'Nancy Lindsay', a more compact variety.

Toxicity

All species of Leucojum are poisonous, as they produce the alkaloids lycorine and galantamine.
